Video Game projects

In the beginning I got into computer science to make video games. Clearly I think there's a little bit of that in everyone that becomes a computer science major. But I don't want to work on video games professionally and the stars did not align for me to work on some of these ideas for money. Im not with the right company and I dont have the right to work with certain IP. My favorite games of all time are League of Legends and Kingdom Hearts. Working on those games or with Riot or Square Enix would be the dream of a life time but I have accepted that I am not growing in the direction that they need to work on their video games. Before I went away to college I spent some time with my good highschool friends talking about what we would do if we worked on some games. Thats where these ideas come from. Pre-Computer science degree with post computer science degree knowledge.

For my software development class I worked in a group of five people to make a video game in the Unity game engine. I prefer the Unreal Engine 4 but Unity is a big name in game development too. The game utilized a singleton design pattern so there is one class that is called one time to manage the entire game. The development life cycle was a combination of agile and spiral as we added, removed, and modified features of the game as we developed it. The entire game was documented in IEEE documents an SRS, an SDS, and test cases. we used Github for version cotnrol. This was my first introduction to video game development and I think it went off pretty sucessfully. I learned a lot of other useful skills for developing software but the leap I made in the unity environment made my ideas for making these games a LOT more doable.
